# Quantum Chemistry: Excited State Simulation

## 📌 Overview
This project simulates ground and excited state energies of molecular systems using quantum algorithms.  
It demonstrates how quantum computing can be applied to problems in spectroscopy, photochemistry, material science, and molecular stability analysis.

---

## 🚀 Use Cases

### 1. Optical Absorption & Emission
- Computed ground and excited state energies of H₂.
- Calculated transition energies.
- Analyzed photon absorption and emission.

### 2. Photochemical Reactions
- Identified excited states that can initiate chemical reactions.
- Compared energy differences between states.

### 3. Band Gap Approximation
- Estimated band gap using the energy difference between ground and first excited state.

### 4. Quantum Algorithm Benchmarking
- Compared classical exact results with VQE.
- Analyzed accuracy and convergence.

### 5. Chemical Bonding & Stability
- Studied energy gaps to understand bond strength and stability.
